Spaghetti Carbonara

Creamy and delicious pasta.

Ingredients

  • 200g spaghetti
  • 100g pancetta or guanciale, diced
  • 2 large eggs
  • 50g Pecorino Romano cheese, grated
  • 50g Parmesan cheese, grated
  • 2 cloves garlic, finely chopped (optional)
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Salt
  • 1 tbsp olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Spaghetti:

    Boil a large pot of salted water and cook the spaghetti according to package instructions until al dente. Reserve a cup of the pasta water before draining.

  2. Prepare the Sauce:

    While the pasta is cooking, whisk together the eggs, Pecorino Romano, and Parmesan in a bowl. Season with a generous amount of black pepper.

  3. Cook the Pancetta:

    Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the pancetta (or guanciale) and cook until crispy. Optionally, add garlic and cook until fragrant.

  4. Combine:

    Add the drained pasta to the skillet with pancetta. Toss to coat the pasta in the fat.

    Remove the skillet from heat and quickly pour in the egg and cheese mixture, stirring vigorously. The heat from the pasta will cook the eggs, creating a creamy sauce. If the sauce is too thick, add a bit of the reserved pasta water until it reaches the desired consistency.

  5. Serve:

    Serve immediately, garnished with extra cheese and black pepper.

Chicken Curry

Spicy and flavorful curry.

Ingredients

  • 500g boneless chicken thighs or breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp ginger, minced
  • 2 tbsp curry powder
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 can (400ml) coconut milk
  • 1 can (400g) diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish
  • Cooked rice or naan for serving

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Aromatics:

    Heat the vegetable oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onions and cook until softened and golden brown. Add the garlic and ginger and cook for another minute.

  2. Spice it Up:

    Stir in the curry powder, cumin, coriander, turmeric, and paprika. Cook for about 1-2 minutes until the spices are fragrant.

  3. Add Chicken:

    Add the chicken pieces to the pot and cook until they are no longer pink on the outside.

  4. Simmer:

    Bring the curry to a boil, then reduce the heat to a simmer. Cook for about 20-25 minutes, or until the chicken is fully cooked and the sauce has thickened.

  5. Season and Serve:

    Season with salt and pepper to taste. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve with cooked rice or naan.

Vegetable Stir Fry

Quick and healthy stir fry.

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 carrot, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 cup snap peas
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp ginger, minced
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp hoisin sauce
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch mixed with 2 tbsp water (optional, for thickening)
  • Cooked rice or noodles for serving
  • Sesame seeds for garnish
  • Sliced green onions for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Vegetables:

    Have all vegetables sliced and ready to go before you start cooking.

  2. Cook the Vegetables:

    Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add the garlic and ginger and stir-fry for about 30 seconds until fragrant.

    Add the broccoli and carrots first as they take longer to cook. Stir-fry for about 2-3 minutes.

  3. Add Remaining Vegetables:

    Add the bell peppers and snap peas to the skillet. Continue to stir-fry for another 2-3 minutes until all vegetables are tender-crisp.

  4. Create the Sauce:

    In a small bowl, combine soy sauce, hoisin sauce, and sesame oil. Pour over the vegetables and stir to coat evenly. If you want a thicker sauce, add the cornstarch mixture at this point and cook for an additional minute until the sauce thickens.

  5. Serve:

    Serve immediately over cooked rice or noodles. Garnish with sesame seeds and sliced green onions.
